About

Hello!

I'm a bioinformatician at Queen Mary University of London, working on epigenetic dysregulation in the brain tumour glioblastoma. My research interests span mathematical biology, statistical modelling and computational simulation. Along the way, I've taken a few fun detours: a degree in chemistry, a DPhil in mathematical biology, a year spent as a risk analyst and several years working on predicting spatiotemporal crime patterns at UCL.

My weapon of choice is Python, but I tolerate R and C++ when called upon. Inside and outside of work, I like messing around with new development frameworks and technology. For that reason, I decided to build this site from scratch and host it on my own VPS using Docker for deployment. I am therefore *directly responsible* for every annoying glitch.

One day, I intend to create (and maybe even populate) a blog...
