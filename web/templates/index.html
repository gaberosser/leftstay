About

Hello!

I'm a bioinformatician at Queen Mary University of London, working on the epigenetics of glioblastoma multiforme. My research interests span mathematical biology, statistical modelling and computational simulation. Along the way, I've taken a few fun detours: a degree in chemistry, a DPhil in mathematical biology, a year spent as a risk analyst and several years working on predicting spatiotemporal crime patterns at UCL.

My weapon of choice is Python, but I tolerate R and C++ when called upon. Inside and outside of work, I like messing around with new development frameworks and technology. For that reason, I decided to build this site from scratch and host it on my own VPS using Docker for deployment. I am therefore *directly responsible* for every annoying glitch.

One day, I want to do a bit of blogging...

Bacterial motility

I worked on bacterial motility for my DPhil. It was my first proper introduction to research in the field of mathematical biology, so I'll always think very fondly of it. My thesis is (somewhat vaguely) entitled "Mathematical modelling and analysis of aspects of planktonic bacterial motility".

The main focus is the fascinating means by which lone bacteria propel themselves through liquid media. Much of my work centred around a new, high-throughput microscopic tracking dataset that had recently been developed by Dr David Wilkinson. Coupled with a very effective piece of tracking software by Dr Trevor Wood, we had a fantastic new dataset to probe the trajectories traced out by freely swimming bacteria such as Rhodobacter sphaeroides. I also had the pleasure of working with my colleague and friend Dr Kit Yates on some of the analysis.

I developed a novel approach to analysing the tracks obtained and applied various mathematical modelling techniques to tackle challenging problems including: "what is the effect of the microscope camera sampling rate on the observed tracks?" and "does R. sphaeroides reorientate lazily, by harnessing Brownian motion?".

  • Date: 2009-2013
  • Location: Wolfson Centre for Mathematical Biology
  • Supervisors: Prof Philip Maini - Dr Ruth Baker - Dr Alex Fletcher
  • Collaborators: Dr Kit Yates - Dr David Wilkinson - Dr Trevor Wood
